Dear <>,
This Shabbat, we are entering the final month of the year, Elul. Elul stands for "ani l'dodi v'dodi li", I am to my beloved and my beloved is to me, referring to the relationship of the Jewish people and G-d, the source of all relationships that exist here on Earth.

Regardless of how the year started or unfolded, we can always finalize the year having a strong relationship between ourselves and G-d and close the year with a profit and positively.
In this week's Torah portion Re'eh, we read that Moses tells the Jewish people to see that G-d has placed before you blessings and curses. As the Torah says elsewhere, G-d creates life and death, Good and evil and it is our choice to choose life over death and good over evil and blessing above curses.

Judaism doesn't just implore us to choose blessing as it's obvious, who would choose a curse over a blessing, death over life and evil over good? 99% of the people would choose the right thing. Yet, Judaism warns us that not always is it apparent what is a blessing and what is a curse, life and death, good and evil. Usually it depends on what happens at the end not what you feel at the moment you're engaged in the choice that you made.
